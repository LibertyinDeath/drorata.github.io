Research highlights

Parameterization in the configuration space

The notion of a configuration space naturally arises in the study of the “piano movers problem” — each point in this space corresponds to a unique placement of the piano. The contribution: a simple and geometrically motivated characterization and parameterization of the points in the configuration space that corresponds to a contact between the piano and the obstacles scattered in the room.

References:

  • D. Atariah and G. Rote, “Configuration space visualization”, Proceedings of the twenty-eighth annual symposium on Computational geometry, [video] [paper] [BibTeX]
  • D. Atariah, S. Ghosh and G. Rote, “On the Parameterization and the Geometry of the Configuration Space of a Single Planar Robot”, Journal of WSCG 2013 [paper] [BiBTeX]
  • D. Atariah, S. Ghosh and Günter Rote, On the Parameterization and the Geometry of the Configuration Space of a Single Planar Robot 2013 [Tech report] [BiBTeX]

Local Approximations of Hyperbolic Paraboloids

Surfaces of negative (Gaussian) curvature constitute a prominent class of surfaces, thus, their approximations are of central importance. A simple subclass of these surfaces is the “hyperbolic paraboloid surfaces” (a.k.a. saddle surfaces). The contribution: formalization of a local optimal (non-interpolating) approximation of saddle surfaces.

References:

  • D. Atariah, G. Rote and M. Wintraecken, “Optimal Triangulation of Saddle Surfaces”, Arxiv 2015 [link]

Contribution to CGAL (Sep. 2012 till July 2014)

Improved and restructured the implementation of computing arrangements of polylines in the 2D Arrangements package of the Computational Geometry Algorithms Library (www.cgal.org). This project involved generic programming in C++, reviewing test suites, using the version control system git and editing documentation using Doxygen.
